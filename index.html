Quadratic Expansion

f(x) = ax² + bx + c
The standard form of a quadratic equation. The discriminant (Δ = b² - 4ac) determines the nature of the roots. Essential for modeling projectile motion in physics.

Logarithmic Rules

log_b(x) + log_b(y) = log_b(xy)
Used to simplify exponential growth models and solve complex power-based equations. These rules form the basis for logarithmic scales like decibels and pH.

Pythagorean Identity

a² + b² = c²
Relates the squares of the sides of a right-angled triangle. This is the cornerstone of trigonometry and Cartesian geometry.

Surface Area of a Cylinder

A = 2πrh + 2πr²
Calculates the total external area of a cylinder including the lateral surface and the two circular bases.
