Quadratic Formula

x = [-b ± √(b²-4ac)] / 2a
The ultimate tool for finding intercepts of parabolas. The discriminant (b²-4ac) tells you if you have 2 real roots, 1 root, or imaginary solutions. Essential for higher-level trajectory modeling.

Logarithm Identity

log(x/y) = log x - log y
The Quotient Rule of Logarithms. This allows you to break down complex division into simple subtraction, making it a cornerstone for calculus and engineering calculations.

Point-Slope Form

y - y₁ = m(x - x₁)
The most efficient way to write a linear equation when you only have one point and the slope. It represents the rate of change relative to a specific starting position.

Pythagorean Theorem

a² + b² = c²
The foundation of Euclidean geometry. It proves that in a right triangle, the square of the longest side is equal to the sum of the squares of the other sides. Perfect for distance calculations in 2D space.

Surface Area of Sphere

A = 4πr²
Calculates the total external area of a perfectly round 3D object. Fun fact: This formula is exactly 4 times the area of a flat circle with the same radius.

Law of Sines

a/sinA = b/sinB = c/sinC
A powerful theorem used to solve for sides or angles in ANY triangle, not just right-angled ones. Critical for navigation and structural physics.
