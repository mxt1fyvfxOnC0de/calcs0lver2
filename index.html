Quadratic Formula

x = [-b ± √(b²-4ac)] / 2a
Used to find the roots of a quadratic equation. The term b²-4ac is the discriminant, which determines the number of real solutions.

Logarithm Power Rule

log_b(x^y) = y · log_b(x)
A key property for solving exponential equations by bringing the exponent down as a multiplier.

Slope-Intercept Form

y = mx + b
Defines a straight line where 'm' is the slope (steepness) and 'b' is the y-intercept (where it hits the vertical axis).

Pythagorean Theorem

a² + b² = c²
For any right-angled triangle, the square of the hypotenuse (c) is equal to the sum of the squares of the other two sides.

Sphere Volume

V = 4/3πr³
Calculates the total 3D space inside a sphere. Requires only the radius 'r' to solve.

Law of Cosines

c² = a² + b² - 2ab cos(C)
A generalization of the Pythagorean theorem used for finding sides/angles in non-right triangles.
